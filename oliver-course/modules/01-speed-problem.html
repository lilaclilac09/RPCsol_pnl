Sequential vs. Parallel — the core idea

Think of making calls to an API like sending letters and waiting for replies. The slow way: send one letter, wait for the reply, then send the next.

The Oliver way: send all nine letters at once, then collect all nine replies as they arrive.

What this means for your Solana tools

The Helius free tier caps you at 100,000 credits per month. Every unnecessary second you spend waiting for sequential calls is also credits you are burning while your users stare at a loading spinner.

🐢

Sequential

Calls go one at a time. Total time = sum of every call. Fine for 1-2 requests. Painful at 9+.

🚀

Oliver Pattern

Calls fire simultaneously. Total time = slowest single call. 6-10× faster on real Helius wallets.

🛡️

With Guardrails

A semaphore caps how many calls go out at once so you do not get rate-limited and banned.

💡
The universal insight: I/O is not the bottleneck, waiting is

Your computer can juggle thousands of pending network requests at once — it is just waiting for answers. The Oliver pattern exploits this: fire everything, then collect. This idea applies to any API, database, or web request you will ever build.
